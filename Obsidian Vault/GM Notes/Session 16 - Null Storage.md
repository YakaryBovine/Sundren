The players will dive deep into [[Skofnildr]]'s beard. They'll start falling to their deaths if they go in without their parachute. Once they land, they'll be in the ring of a massive pit, filled to the brim with technology.

In the center is a crater, where the fidget toy they're looking for fell and destroyed the surrounding trinkets. The crater is 4800 kilometres away, so they'll need to use the 200km/h hovertrike to get there. They will face many challenges along the way, none combat related.
## 0. Skofnildr's attention
As the players progress, they catch on to the fact that Skofnildr is trying to stop them. He will peer into the dimension, destabilize matter, dispose of items in his collection, in an attempt to pull them out - but he will refuse to actually enter.

The players have 36 hours to reach the center before Skofnildr destroys them. The journey normally takes 24 hours, so they will be fine as long as they do not incur 12 more hours.
## 1. Problem with the trike sensors
The hovertrike's sensors are heavily disoriented, and believe that the direction of the ground is towards the center, causing it to continously hover towards it when in operation. 

The sensors can be modified with a Know or Craft skill check of 12. Failing that, the players will have to seek out sensors from this location, which they can find with a Survive or Notice skill check of 12. They can try this as many times as they like, but will lose 1 hour of time each time.
## 2. Collection swarm
The items in Skofnildr's collection have grown a deep appreciation for being collected. The player's hovertrike start to get followed by a swarm of items, which they can avoid if them simply never stop. If they do, the items will pile onto the hovertrike, slowing the journey by 4 hours. They can get rid of them through negotiation, such as expressing gratitude or appreciation for a select few trinkets.
## 3. The servant
The players come across an original Warforged robot; distinct from Hosts, it is an actual robot, and has only simple subroutines. As soon as it sees the players, it starts doing what they think they want, which happens to include blocking their path and making their life very difficult.

Attacking it will incur Skofnildr's wrath, while giving it other tasks to do, or being nice to it, will dissuade it away.
## 4. The metamachines
The players way is blocked, and they can only interact with machines that request and store information. It seems they form a huge portion of Skofnildr's memory, and are empty of data. They request that they be filled in.

The players must give them at least 4 pieces of deeply meaningful information to be let through. For every 4 they deliver after that, they can get an hour back on their journey.
## 5. The first worshipper
A woman lounges atop a giant rock, which is in actuality a "prepared core", a prepared arrangement of rocks that could be used to fashion a blade. She won't let the players pass until they identify who she is, and what Skofnildr would want them to do with the core.

The woman is Skofnildr's first and only worshipper; the girl who invented the first piece of technology without assistance, the prepared-core she now sits atop.

Skofnildr enjoys it when technology is used. He would appreciate it most if the players were to fashion a blade using the core.
## 6. The silence band
A fake-out; within this zone, there is no sound whatsoever. There is no threat beyond this, but there is some risk that the players will spend time assessing the problem.
## 7. Echo zone
Players enter a long corridor full of trinkets that interact with sound. Every sound they make, including the trike, risks damage to their ear drums as the soundwaves are amplified.

The players could use technology to modulate the sound, prayer to appeal to it, or song to make it more bearable.

Every failure will incur an hour of time.
## 8. Destabilized crater
The space around the crater is heavily destabilized, making movement through to the center extremely difficult. The players could:
- Use technology and careful piloting to reach the middle
- Pray to Skofnildr

It is imperative that the trinket is removed as carefully as possible, but moving it at all risks breaking all of the trinkets around it. Players must take decisions to be *very very* careful, and may also choose to reassure Skofnildr of their respect and intent.

Failing consumes time.

This is an initiative-encounter.
- Calm Skofnildr with reassuring words (Convince, Pray)
- Stabilize the trinkets in the area (Know, Pray, Craft, Perform) (Perform works because the destabilization here is reflected by a discordant dubstep song)
- Watch for destabilizations (Magic, Pray)
- Navigate to the center (Exert, Sneak)

There are a number of destabilized trinkets.
If the navigator fails their DC8 roll, they step on one and take 2d12 damage.
If the stabilizer fails their roll, a new secret trinket is added to the arena.
If the calmer fails their roll, the players ALL take 2d8 damage.
If the watcher passes their roll, they reveal a secret trinket.

At the end of every round, all players take 1d6 damage, which escalates to 2d6 damage after 4 rounds.
## If the players fail
If the players reach the center after the 24 hours, there will be no way to retrieve the trinket without incurring Skofnildr's wrath. They can do so anyway, as the trinket can be destroyed in one attack, but Skofnildr will destroy them in turn. He will then be freed.
